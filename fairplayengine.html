Product Concept • R&D

Fair Play Lineup

I applied my enterprise design experience to solve the common issues of youth sports. This is a mobile tool built to manage rotations, safety, and fairness in real time.

The Full Workflow.

This walkthrough demonstrates the end-to-end flow of the app. I focused on a "zero friction" transition between setup and game time, ensuring the system does the heavy lifting so the coach can stay focused on the field.

Our Target

Meet Kristin, she represents out target user. Across the country, most recreational leagues are made up of volunteer coaches. These coaches have their hands full trying to balance their full time jobs and the duties of coaching. Thay are busy teaching the kids and often can be unaware that they aren't adhering to the playing time rules for their league or simply miss things during games.

01. Constraint Based Logic

I treated league rules as a logic layer rather than just a settings page. By establishing system constraints early, I de-risked the entire game experience, preventing illegal rotations before they even happen.

02. Managing Volatile Data

Rosters in youth sports are highly dynamic. I designed the roster system to handle last-minute absences and state changes gracefully, allowing the coach to update the active lineup without breaking the underlying math.

03. Reducing Cognitive Load

The live game is where the "Fast Math" happens. I utilized a drag-and-drop pattern (also added click n place for smaller screens) with real-time heuristic feedback, ensuring that the interface does the thinking so the coach can stay present in the game.

04. Closing the Loop

Design doesn't end when the game does. I created post-game reports that verify compliance with fairness rules and highlight player development, providing a transparent record for leagues and parents.

Reflections & Roadmap

While the first iteration of Fair Play Lineup successfully handles the core logic of rotations, I have already identified several opportunities to deepen the product experience. My focus for the next version is to move from a standalone tool to a connected ecosystem.

Iterative Insights

  • → Account Persistence: Building a secure account layer is a priority to allow coaches to save data and access their rosters across different devices.
  • → Interaction Refinement: I am evaluating the tactile friction of player assignments to ensure the UI is even more forgiving during initial lineup creation and mid-game chaos.
  • → Environmental Testing: I plan to conduct outdoor "field tests" to fine-tune the color contrast and ensure maximum legibility in direct sunlight.

Future Considerations

  • → Season Support: Moving beyond single games to track player development, playing time, positions played, and attendance over an entire year.
  • → Multi-Coach Sync: Enabling real-time updates so assistants and head coaches stay in perfect alignment.
  • → Centralized Rules: Allowing leagues to set global fairness rules that teams automatically adopt.
  • → Coaches Notes: A place for private observations that can be toggled on or off during parent reporting.
  • → League Integration: Automating the "paperwork" by syncing directly with league roster databases.
  • → Performance Data: Integrating with scoring apps to help older age groups balance performance with fair play.

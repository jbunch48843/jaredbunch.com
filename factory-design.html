Case Study

Factory Design Vision

I led the vision for a new way to collaboratively plan large factories. The goal was to make the whole process faster and more accurate for teams working all over the world.

The Problem

Teams are working in their own separate groups. Because they are not sharing data well, it leads to really expensive mistakes when it comes time to actually build the factory.

My Role

I was the Principal Designer. I spent my time getting different global leaders on the same page and defining a vision for what we wanted to achieve with the new system.

The Result

The vision was to cut planning time by at least 65% and move from scattered data to a unified, single source of truth, that would become a global standard for the company.

Finding the current state

I started with a deep evaluation of how the software was addressing the customers needs. I sat with customers to see where the existing tools were helping and where they were falling short.

While the software solved the immediate technical needs, I identified several major opportunities to improve the handoff between teams. This was the key to reducing that 65% planning lag.

Current Issues Addressed

  • Problem:

    Customers need to manage 2D and 3D representations of their facilities independently.

  • Solution:

    Our ability to sync between 2D and 3D representations, keeping them in-sync and automatically managing the relationship between them.

  • Problem:

    Costly space and interference issues encountered at build/install time.

  • Solution:

    Our ability to aggregate various data points and perform interference checks virtually during the design phase helps to catch these issues way before build/install and avoid costly mistakes.

  • Problem:

    Overall efficiency.

  • Solution:

    All of our various layout tools and automations address many issues, but likely not all.

  • Problem:

    Data management - managing all of the various pieces of data from factory layouts and assets.

  • Solution:

    Our integration with Vault addresses these issues (with room for improvement).

  • Problem:

    Many customers today rely on spreadsheets with formulas, completely disconnected from their layout designs.

  • Solution:

    Process Analysis helps them move out of spreadsheets and provides simulation and real-time analysis.

Identified Key Opportunities

  • → Centralize technical data to prevent silos and facilitate true multi-user workflows.
  • → Create a real time feedback loop for global teams.
  • → Provide a true data sync with conflict resolution.

Defining the Vision

To keep focused on the "Why," we developed several Problem Statements. These became our north star during the design phase.

"In what ways might we enable collaboration and better communication?"

"In what ways might we help customer complete their projects faster, more efficiently, and with fewer errors?"

"In what ways might we allow customers to reuse their data?"

Storyboarding the Vision

I used storyboards to walk the leadership team through the big picture vision. This was about the story of transforming the factory design process, not just screens.

Final thoughts

The work and illustrations shared here are a sampling of the broader vision I developed for the Factory Design Utilities product. I spent months surveying and interviewing customers to ensure our plan was grounded in reality.

The value shown in this vision was clear enough that it led to the creation and delivery of the Factory Common Data Environment. This provided the foundation for everything else we planned to build.

While the full vision was not realized due to shifts in company priorities, the project remains a great example of how deep research and strategy can move a product into a cloud-first era.
